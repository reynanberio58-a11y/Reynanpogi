About Gattaran

Gattaran is a municipality in the province of Cagayan in northern Philippines, part of the Cagayan Valley. It is known for its wide agricultural lands and peaceful rural communities. The town is located near the Cagayan River, the longest river in the country, which supports farming and fishing in the area. Most residents rely on agriculture, with major crops such as rice, corn, tobacco, and vegetables. Gattaran also has natural scenery like rivers, farmlands, hills, and forests. The people are mostly Ilocano and Ibanag, known for their culture, hospitality, and community traditions. Life in Gattaran is generally quiet and community-centered, with schools, churches, and local markets playing an important role in everyday life..
